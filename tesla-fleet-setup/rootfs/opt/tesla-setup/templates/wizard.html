Generate Security Keys

Tesla requires a cryptographic key pair to verify your Home Assistant instance. This is generated automatically — just click the button below.

What does this do? It creates a unique pair of security keys. The "public" key will be shared with Tesla so they know it's really your Home Assistant talking to them. The "private" key stays safely on your device.

Make Your Public Key Reachable

Tesla's servers need to download your public key over the internet. We'll figure out the best way to do this for your setup.

Tesla needs to verify your public key over the internet. We'll create a free temporary tunnel using Cloudflare — no account needed. It only stays active during this setup and shuts down when you're done.
What is a Cloudflare tunnel?

A Cloudflare tunnel creates a secure, temporary link from the internet to this add-on. Tesla uses it to verify your public key during setup.

It does not expose your Home Assistant. Only the public key endpoint is accessible, and the tunnel shuts down after setup.

Even if you have Nabu Casa, the tunnel is needed because Tesla must reach this add-on directly to verify the key.

Troubleshooting: test fails?

Using Nabu Casa? Make sure Remote Access is enabled in Settings → Home Assistant Cloud.

Using a reverse proxy (nginx/Caddy)? The path /.well-known/appkeys must be proxied through to Home Assistant.

Using the tunnel? It may take 10-15 seconds to become reachable. Try clicking "Test Reachability" again.

Register Your App with Tesla

This is the one step that needs to be done on Tesla's website. Follow these instructions carefully — we've pre-filled everything for you.

Time needed: About 2-3 minutes. You'll need to sign in with the same Tesla account that's linked to your car.
  1. Open developer.tesla.com in a new tab and sign in with your Tesla account This is the same email/password you use for the Tesla app on your phone
  2. If this is your first time, you may need to accept the Developer Terms. Just check the box and click Submit.
  3. Click "Create App" to start the Tesla registration wizard. You'll see a 4-step form. Fill in the Application Details page:
    Application Name Home Assistant
    Description Home Assistant Tesla Fleet Integration
    Purpose of Usage Personal home automation with Home Assistant
    Then click Next.
  4. On the Client Details page, select "Authorization Code and Machine-to-Machine" (the left option), then paste your URLs:
    Allowed Origin URL
    Allowed Redirect URI
    Leave "Allowed Returned URL(s)" empty — it's optional Then click Next.
  5. On the API & Scopes page, tick the permissions you need: Recommended: Profile Information, Vehicle Information, Vehicle Location, Vehicle Commands, and Vehicle Charging Management.
    If you have a Powerwall, also tick Energy Product Information and Energy Product Commands.     Then click Next. Skip the Billing Details page (click Submit).
  6. After submitting, Tesla shows your Client ID and Client Secret. Copy both into the fields below. The Client Secret is only shown once! Save it somewhere safe if you want a backup.
    Note: Tesla may show this page in your browser's language. The two fields you need are the UUID (Client ID) and the masked dots (Client Secret — click the eye icon to reveal it).

Verify with Tesla

This step tells Tesla about your app and lets them verify your public key. It happens automatically — just click the button.

What happens: We send your app credentials to Tesla. Tesla then connects to your public key URL to verify everything matches. This usually takes a few seconds.

Troubleshooting: verification failed?

"Public key not found" — Tesla couldn't reach your /.well-known/appkeys URL. Go back to Step 2 and re-test reachability.

"Invalid client" — Double-check your Client ID and Client Secret. Go back to Step 3 to re-enter them.

Timeout or network error — Tesla's API may be temporarily slow. Wait a minute and try again.

Connect Your Tesla Account

Last step! Sign in with your Tesla account and give Home Assistant permission to access your vehicle data.

What happens: A new window will open with Tesla's sign-in page. After you log in and approve access, you'll be redirected back here automatically.
Important: If you're using the temporary tunnel, do not close this browser tab or stop the add-on until the sign-in is complete.
